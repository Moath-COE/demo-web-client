# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Sanad primarily serves Saudi students studying in Arabic, on mobile and desktop, inside an RTL learning experience. Their main job is to understand course material, move through enrolled lessons, ask questions during study sessions, and get help without leaving the learning context.

Secondary surfaces include the public landing page, legal pages, enrollment, and administration. These should support the product, not redefine the default design register.

## Product Purpose

Sanad is an Arabic-first AI tutor for structured course learning. It gives students a guided study workspace with course libraries, chapter materials, PDF-based study sessions, voice or AI assistance, topic guidance, and progress-oriented navigation.

Success means students can start a session quickly, understand what to do next, ask for help at the right moment, and return to their course without confusion or visual friction.

## Brand Personality

Warm, present, encouraging.

Sanad should feel like a capable tutor leaning in beside the student: warm enough that questions feel safe and welcome, present enough to make the next step obvious, and clear enough to keep attention on the lesson. It is human and inviting, never cold or clinical.

The visual identity is coral, navy, and cream. Coral is the tutor's voice — confident and encouraging on actions, progress, and guidance. Navy holds focus and trust. Cream is the warm page the lesson is written on.

## Anti-references

Sanad should not look or sound like a childish tutor app. Avoid cartoonish cues, toy-like controls, excessive gamification, and mascot-led interfaces that make older students feel talked down to.

Sanad should not feel like a dense admin dashboard. Avoid heavy tables, cramped control panels, jargon-heavy labels, and cold operational layouts on student-facing screens.

Sanad should not feel clinical or sterile. Avoid cold gray-only palettes, flat blue-on-white edtech templates, and detached, institutional tones. Sanad is warm and human.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the study task primary. Navigation, assistance, and visual treatment should reduce interruption during a session.
2. Make the next action obvious. A student should know whether to continue, ask, enroll, open a chapter, or return without scanning the whole page.
3. Lead with warmth and confidence. Use coral for action and guidance, warm surfaces for reading space, and clear hierarchy — not decorative excitement or cold austerity.
4. Respect the learning surface. Course material, PDF content, and AI guidance should feel integrated, not like competing panels.
5. Preserve Arabic-first behavior. RTL, Arabic copy, and Saudi student context are core product requirements, not localization afterthoughts.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Arabic RTL correctness is required across layouts, navigation, forms, overlays, and responsive states. Never apply letter-spacing to Arabic text; it breaks cursive letter-joining.

Default work should favor high comfort: readable Arabic text, clear contrast (body text ≥4.5:1), visible focus states, predictable controls, reduced distraction, and motion that does not interfere with study. The product follows the system color-scheme preference (warm cream light surface, navy dark surface). When animation is used, it must respect reduced-motion preferences.
